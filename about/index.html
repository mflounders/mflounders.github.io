Welcome

I'm a medical student interested in the intersection of medicine, neuroscience, and mental health. I live in Philadelphia, PA and love to run. In my future, I aspire to be a doctor who writes and responds to code - always learning along the way

Research

My research is focused on understanding the brain with non-invasive multi-modal imaging. During my post-baccalaureate fellowship with Biyu He, I studied visual perception using fMRI and MEG methods and multivariate pattern analysis. Recently, I am applying neuroimaging analysis in clinical settings to develop individualized treatments for depression under the mentorship of Desmond Oathes.


Highlighted Project

ASLPrep: A Generalizable Platform for Processing of Arterial Spin Labeled MRI and Quantification of Regional Brain Perfusion
led by Azeez Adebimpe and PennLINC team
Arterial spin labeled (ASL) magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is the primary method for non-invasively measuring regional brain perfusion in humans. We introduce ASLPrep, a suite of software pipelines that ensure the reproducible and generalizable processing of ASL MRI data
